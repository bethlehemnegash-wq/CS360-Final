# CS 360 Portfolio Reflection

## Briefly summarize the requirements and goals of the app you developed. What user needs was this app designed to address?

For this course, I created a weight tracker app. The goal was to give users a simple way to record their weight, track their progress, and stay motivated over time. I designed the app for people who want an easy way to monitor their weight without having to use a complicated fitness app with a lot of extra features.

## What screens and features were necessary to support user needs and produce a user-centered UI for the app? How did your UI designs keep users in mind? Why were your designs successful?

The app included a login screen, a weight entry screen, a progress screen, and notifications. When designing the app, I focused on making it simple and easy to navigate. I wanted users to be able to quickly enter their information and view their progress without having to search through multiple screens. Keeping the layout consistent throughout the app helped create a better user experience and made the app easier to use.

## How did you approach the process of coding your app? What techniques or strategies did you use? How could those techniques or strategies be applied in the future?

I approached coding one feature at a time instead of trying to build everything at once. I would complete a feature, test it, fix any issues, and then move on to the next part of the app. Breaking the project into smaller pieces made the development process much more manageable. This is a strategy I will continue to use in future projects because it helps keep things organized and makes troubleshooting easier.

## How did you test to ensure your code was functional? Why is this process important, and what did it reveal?

I tested the app frequently in the Android Emulator while building each feature. I tested the login functionality, database storage, notifications, and navigation between screens to make sure everything worked correctly. Testing helped me catch issues early and showed me where improvements were needed before moving on to the next stage of development.

## Consider the full app design and development process from initial planning to finalization. Where did you have to innovate to overcome a challenge?

One challenge I faced was implementing the database and making sure user information was stored correctly. It took some trial and error to get everything working as expected. I overcame this by breaking the problem into smaller steps and testing along the way. Once everything was connected, I had a much better understanding of how data storage works in mobile applications.

## In what specific component of your mobile app were you particularly successful in demonstrating your knowledge, skills, and experience?

I was most successful with the database functionality because it allowed users to save and access their weight data. This was one of the most important features of the app and brought together many of the skills I learned throughout the course. Seeing the app successfully store and retrieve data made the project feel complete and showed how much I had learned during the development process.
